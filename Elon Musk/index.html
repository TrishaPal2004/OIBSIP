Biography

Elon Musk, born on June 28, 1971, is a renowned businessman and investor known for his leadership in various groundbreaking ventures. He is the founder, chairman, CEO, and CTO of SpaceX, pioneering private space exploration. Additionally, Musk served as the former CEO and product architect of Tesla, Inc., transforming the automotive industry with electric vehicles. He is the owner, executive chairman, and CTO of X Corp., overseeing diverse technological endeavors. Musk founded The Boring Company, focusing on tunnel construction and transportation projects. He also founded xAI, dedicated to advancing artificial intelligence with a focus on safety, and co-founded Neuralink, developing brain-computer interface technologies. Moreover, Musk is a co-founder of OpenAI, promoting friendly AI research for global benefit. He serves as the president of the Musk Foundation, leading philanthropic efforts in renewable energy, space, and education. Musk is among the world's wealthiest individuals, with an estimated net worth exceeding $190 billion, primarily from his stakes in Tesla and SpaceX. His ventures have revolutionized industries and inspired innovation worldwide.
